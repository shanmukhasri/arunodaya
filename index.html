About the Organization

Association for Welfare And Comprehensive Rural Development (AWARD) is a registered nonprofit organization dedicated to the welfare of individuals with developmental disabilities.

Our focus is on providing special education for children with developmental disabilities such as Cerebral Palsy, Down's Syndrome, Autism, and Mental Challenges.

Currently, over 40+ children are benefitting from various therapy services provided through our school program.

Organization’s Motto

Our motto is to raise awareness in society about the challenges faced by special children due to various socio-economic factors.

We extend our hands to support these children in every possible way, empowering them to live independently and with dignity.

We give voice to their rights, becoming their wings, allowing them to soar and pursue their dreams.

“We see them as differently abled, not disabled, because they are Special.”

Chairperson’s Voice

Life is so beautiful and precious. Like how the day and night come along one after the other, in life, beautiful times and bad times come along. We shouldn’t lose hope and faith in ourselves to come over our bad times to see a beautiful tomorrow. One thing is for sure: no matter what, time keeps changing, and everything that happens does so for a reason.

"On this note, I would like to share one of my personal experiences of a dark night and how I came out of that bitter darkness to see a brighter tomorrow."

Many years ago, my life took an unexpected turn with the birth of my son. Like any other parents, we were basking in joy with our newborn, only to be told by the doctors that our baby was not typical. Our son was diagnosed with Cerebral Palsy and declared Partially Sightless with Nystagmus and Corneal Opacity. At just 19, my ordinary world was plunged into darkness. I struggled to even understand what the doctors were saying, let alone pronounce those unfamiliar terms.

Each passing day brought two realities: my baby’s condition and society’s harsh response. Unfortunately, his condition worsened, and I found myself helpless. Although he is no longer with us, my son gave me a profound purpose. In his memory, “Arunodaya Mano Vikas Kendram” was born in 1994, in association with the Association for Welfare and Comprehensive Rural Development (AWARD).

Recovery was a slow, challenging process. During this time, I saw other families enduring similar pain. I realized the lack of public awareness about these conditions and the social stigma against children with special needs. Determined to make a difference, I resumed my studies, which had been interrupted by marriage, and joined the Thakur Hari Prasad Institute of Research and Rehabilitation for the Mentally Handicapped in Rajahmundry.

I completed a diploma course in Mental Retardation (D.S.E - MR) and continuously updated my knowledge through various seminars and workshops, including those hosted by the National Institute for the Mentally Handicapped – Secunderabad, Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) at Rastriya Seva Samiti (RASS) – Tirupati, and disability rights initiatives by the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, Government of India.

My sole purpose is to support special children and their families, sparing them the pain I once endured.

"Sarve Jana Sukhinobhavanthu" – May all beings be happy.
